Vietnamese Pickles — Drain Before Use
Portion size
30g drained pickles per portion
How to prep
Scoop pickles from jar into a small strainer. Drain for at least 5 minutes. Transfer drained pickles into a container in the display fridge. Never put the whole jar on the counter.
Storage (opened jar)
2–8°C, lid sealed. Use within 7 days of opening.
⚠️ Pickles with excess liquid are the #1 cause of soggy bread. Always drain thoroughly before adding to the bánh mì.

Assembly Steps — made to order
1
Warm the bread in the oven
3–4 min
Place 1 roll in the toaster oven at 170–180°C, top+bottom heat. Bake 3–4 minutes until crust is evenly crispy. Do not over-bake — the inside must stay soft and airy.
💡 Check: gently squeeze the roll — the crust should make a small crackle sound. Inside must still be soft and warm.
2
Cut the bread with scissors
5 sec
Place the warm roll flat on the cutting board. Use kitchen scissors to cut lengthwise, ¾ of the way through. Open like a book — both halves remain connected at the bottom.
⚠️ Do not cut all the way through. Scissors give a cleaner cut than a knife, especially on hot bread.
3
Spread pâté and butter
10 sec
Open the roll on the cutting board:
→ Top half (inner side): spread 20g pâté evenly from end to end.
→ Bottom half (inner side): spread 15g butter evenly from end to end.
Use the back of a spoon or spreader. Spread all the way to the edges.
💡 Vietnamese rule: pâté on one side, butter on the other. Never mix. Never skip the butter — it adds richness and prevents the meat from tasting dry.
4
Layer the cold cuts (on the butter side)
15 sec
On the butter side, layer in order:
① 3 slices ham (~30g) — spread evenly along the full length.
② 3 slices cha lua (~30g) — place on top of the ham.
Use tongs — never bare hands.
5
Add vegetables and pickles
10 sec
① 4 cucumber slices (~20g) — arranged along the bread.
② 30g drained pickles — spooned evenly on top.
③ 5g cilantro — scattered over the top.
⚠️ Pickles must be fully drained. Check the container — if liquid has pooled at the bottom, drain again before use.
6
Season with Maggi
3 sec
Drop 5–6 drops (~3ml) of Maggi along the length of the bread. Ask the customer: "Would you like chili?" — add 2–3 thin slices only if requested.
7
Package and serve
5 sec
Close the roll gently. Slide into the paper bag (opening facing up). Add 2 napkins inside. Place into the plastic carry bag and tie loosely. Hand to customer immediately.
⚠️ Maximum time from assembly to customer handoff: 5 minutes. Never pre-assemble bánh mì — bread loses its crunch within 10–15 minutes.
Quality Check
✓
Crust is crispy and warm — not soft or damp
✓
Pâté and butter spread evenly to all edges — both layers visible when opened
✓
Meat covers the full length of the bread, not bunched in one spot
✓
Pickles are drained — no liquid pooling inside the bread
✓

✓

✗

✗

Bánh Mì Trứng
Vietnamese Fried Egg Sandwich — Crispy-edged sunny-side up egg
⏱ 4–5 min☀️ Great for breakfastRequires frying
🍳 Vietnamese fried egg (trứng ốp la) is defined by its crispy lacy edges and runny yolk. It is fried in hot oil — NOT butter. The crispy edge is what makes it authentically Vietnamese.
Ingredient amounts — 1 portion

Step 1 — Fry the egg (run parallel with bread warming)
1
Heat the 4-hole pan and oil
45–60 sec
Place the 4-hole egg pan on the induction cooktop at heat level 7–8/10 (high). Add 5ml oil into each hole you will use. Wait until the oil is hot and shimmering.
💡 Temperature test: drop a tiny splash of water into the oil — if it sizzles immediately, the oil is ready. Cold oil = no crispy edges.
2
Warm bread at the same time
Parallel
While the pan heats, place the roll in the toaster oven at 170–180°C. Time them to finish together.
3
Crack egg into each hole
90–120 sec
Crack 1 egg per hole directly into the hot oil. The white should immediately sizzle and start crisping at the edges. Sprinkle a small pinch of salt and white pepper over the white. Cook until:
→ White is fully cooked (no transparent parts)
→ Edges are golden and lacy/crispy
→ Yolk remains runny
⚠️ Do NOT flip the egg. Do NOT cover the pan. Vietnamese fried egg is always sunny-side up. The runny yolk breaks when the customer bites in — that is the intended experience.
Step 2 — Assembly
4
Cut and spread bread
10 sec
Remove bread from oven. Cut ¾ through with scissors. Spread 15g pâté on top half, 15g butter on bottom half.
5
Place egg and toppings
15 sec
① Slide the fried egg gently from the pan onto the butter side of the bread.
② Add 3 cucumber slices alongside the egg.
③ Spoon 25g drained pickles on top.
④ Sprinkle 5g spring onion.
⑤ Drop 5–6 drops of Maggi.
💡 Slide the egg gently — do not break the yolk. An intact yolk is part of the presentation.
6
Package and serve
5 sec
Fold the roll gently (do not press hard — will break the yolk). Place in paper bag + napkins + plastic bag. Tell the customer: "The yolk is runny — that's the Vietnamese style, it's delicious!"
Quality Check
✓
Egg white fully cooked — no transparent or raw-looking areas
✓
Edges golden and lacy/crispy (not pale or flat)
✓
Yolk still runny — not hard-cooked
✓
Bread crust crispy and warm
✗
Yolk fully cooked/hard → oil too hot or cooked too long — reduce heat and time
✗
White edges pale, not crispy → oil not hot enough — wait longer before adding egg

💡 No pâté for this item. Spread butter on both sides of bread. The lemongrass chicken flavour is the star — pâté would overpower it.
Step 1 — Grill the chicken (to order)
1
Heat the grill pan
1–2 min
Place grill pan on induction cooktop at heat level 8–9/10. Spray 5ml oil evenly over the ridges. Wait until the pan is very hot — a drop of water should evaporate instantly on contact.
💡 A properly hot pan is critical. If the pan is not hot enough, the chicken will stick to the ridges and not develop grill marks.
2
Place chicken slices on pan
—
Using tongs, take 120g marinated chicken from the fridge. Lay slices in a single layer — do not overlap. Place on the hot grill pan — it should sizzle immediately.
⚠️ Never stack chicken slices. Overlapping pieces will not cook evenly and will not develop grill marks.
3
Grill side 1
90 sec
Do not touch or move the chicken for the first 90 seconds. After 90 seconds, lift one piece with tongs to check the underside — it should show clear golden-brown grill marks. If not yet marked, wait another 20–30 seconds.
4
Flip — Grill side 2
60–90 sec
Flip each piece with tongs. Grill for another 60–90 seconds. Check doneness: cut through the thickest piece — inside must be completely white, no pink. Or use food thermometer: ≥75°C inside.
⚠️ Any pink inside = not fully cooked. Continue grilling. Never serve undercooked chicken.
5
Rest chicken
30 sec
Remove from pan to the cutting board. Let rest 30 seconds before placing in bread — prevents steam from making the bread soggy.
Step 2 — Assembly (run parallel: warm bread while grilling)
6
Warm bread and spread butter
Parallel with steps 3–4
Place roll in oven while grilling. Cut ¾ through with scissors. Spread 10g butter on top half + 10g butter on bottom half (total 20g, both sides).
7
Layer chicken and toppings
15 sec
① Place 90g grilled chicken along the bread using tongs.
② Add 4 cucumber slices (~20g).
③ Spoon 30g drained pickles.
④ Add 5g cilantro.
⑤ Drop 5–6 drops of Maggi (~3ml).
8
Package and serve immediately
5 sec
Paper bag + napkins + plastic carry bag. Serve within 3 minutes of grilling — grilled chicken loses its aroma quickly when cold.
Quality Check
✓
Chicken is golden-brown with visible grill marks — not pale, not charred black
✓
Cut the thickest piece: completely white inside, no pink
✓
Clear lemongrass aroma when the bread is opened
✓
Bread crust crispy and warm, served within 3 minutes
✗
Pink inside the chicken → keep grilling, never serve
✗
No grill marks, pale colour → pan not hot enough — heat longer before next order

Before Opening — Setup & Prep
30–45 min before
□
Receive baguettes: Accept delivery, check each roll (weight, colour, aroma, no mould). Arrange in bread box away from sunlight.
□
Check display fridge: Verify temperature ≤8°C. Inspect all ingredients — colour, smell, expiry.
□
Get marinated chicken: Remove overnight-marinated chicken from fridge. Check aroma (should smell of lemongrass). Keep sealed in fridge until order comes in.
□
Slice cha lua: Cut at 30–45° angle, 3mm thick. Group in sets of 3 slices (~30g). Label with prep time. Place in fridge.
□
Slice cucumber: Cut at 45°, 4–5mm thick. Store in sealed container. Do not prep more than 2 hours before service.
□
Prepare pickles: Drain pickles from jar into small strainer, 5 min. Transfer drained pickles to a container in the fridge.
□
Wash cilantro: Rinse, shake dry, remove roots. Line container with paper towel. Store in fridge.
□
Check tools: Tongs, spreaders, cutting board — must be clean and dry. Scissors in place.
□
Preheat oven: Turn on toaster oven to 175°C. Allow 5 minutes to reach temperature.
□
Packaging stock: Confirm sufficient paper bags, napkins, and plastic carry bags for the shift.
B
During Service
Made to order
□
Cook to order only: Never pre-assemble. Maximum 3 minutes per portion from order to handoff.
□
Restock check every 30 min: Check all containers in display fridge. Refill when below 30% capacity.
□
Pickles: Pour off any liquid that collects in the pickle container. Pickles must always be dry.
□
Cucumber: If prepped cucumber has been sitting for 2+ hours unused — discard and cut fresh.
□
Chicken: Only remove from fridge when an order comes in. Do not leave marinated chicken out for more than 15 minutes.
□
Continuous hygiene: Wipe down the board and counter after every 5 portions. Rinse tongs with hot water if switching between chicken and other items.
C
End of Shift — Clean & Store
After closing
□
Leftover baguettes: Discard all remaining rolls — do not keep overnight.
□
Prepped cucumber: Discard all — do not store overnight.
□
Cilantro: Discard yellowed or wilted parts. Store remaining fresh cilantro in paper-lined container in fridge for next day.
□
Marinated chicken: If marinated >24 hours — discard. If <24 hours — seal and refrigerate for next day.
□
Sliced cha lua: If prepped >24 hours ago — discard. Otherwise seal and refrigerate.
□
Jars (pâté, butter, pickles): Seal lids tightly. Return to fridge. Label with opening date if not already done.
□
Equipment: Switch off oven and induction cooktop. Let cool 15 minutes then wipe clean.
□
Clean all tools: Wash scissors, tongs, spreaders, cutting board with hot water and dish soap. Air dry.
□
Record keeping: Note portions sold per item + remaining stock. Report any issues to manager.
□
Marinate chicken for tomorrow: If today's chicken was fully used, marinate a fresh batch tonight for best flavour.
⚠️ Food Safety — Non-negotiable rules:
① Wash hands with soap before handling any food, and after touching phone, face, or hair.
② Never use bare hands to handle meat or vegetables — always use tongs.
③ Any perishable food left outside refrigeration for more than 2 hours must be discarded.
④ Chicken that has not reached ≥75°C internally must never be served.
